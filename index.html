"International Move" means that people leave their known environment and have to interact with new entities, often sharing a lot of sensitive information in a short amount of time. Usually, adulting and community help understand how to navigate these. However, when changing the environment radically, these usual pathways to navigate data sharing and whether or not something is a scam may be a lot harder.

Want to Contribute? #

We are still looking for interview participants who have legally moved to the US in 2017 or later. We would love to hear about your experiences throughout the whole moving process.

We are are a group of researchers from the Usable Security & Privacy Lab (GWUSEC) at The George Washington University and are interested in understanding the challenges that international students and professionals who have recently moved to the US face and what measures they undertake to protect their security and privacy. Moreover, we are interested to learn about (security) advice they receive throughout the whole moving process and what security implications this entails.

Information about the Interview #

  • Your data will be de-identified, we may use short quotes from your interview at the most.
  • We estimate that the entire interview will take approximately 60 minutes of your valuable time.
  • The interview may be conducted through any video calling software convenient to both participants and researchers, such as Zoom. No camera will be required for online interviews.
  • You can choose to receive a $20 Amazon gift card as a token of gratitude for your participation.

How to Participate #

  • You can fill in the eligibility survey and directly book a timeslot for the interview via our Calendly event scheduler at the end of the survey.
  • Once you pick a slot and confirm your availabilty you will receive a calendar invitation in the email you provided.
  • The interview itself should not take more than 60 minutes.

Informed Consent and Scheduling

If you have any questions or concerns, please contact Mindy Tran (mindytran@gwu.edu).

Example Questions #

Here are some example questions that we may ask during the interview:

  1. What information and documents were you asked to share to be able to move?
  2. Did you feel unsafe or uncomfortable sharing any documents or information throughout the whole process?
  3. What advice did you receive in terms of security and privacy?

Data Handling #

Our study was approved by IRB and all collected data is handled according to strict GDPR/DSGVO regulations. All collected data will only be accessible by our research team. The focus group sessions and interviews will be audio-recorded and transcripted. This allows us to capture all details and analyze the data better. We are using a transciption service, Amberscript, to transcribe the audio recordings. Here is how we will handle your data:

  • The recording of your interview will be destroyed after we create the transcripts a few days after the interview.
  • Your personally identifiable information including name will not be purposely collected.
  • Since participant names will not be recorded, codes such as P001, P002 etc will be used to report the results.
  • De-identified data may be retained and used in future research.

About Us #

We are a group of researchers from the Usable Security & Privacy Lab (GWUSEC) at The George Washington University. We work on problems related to computer security and privacy from a human centered approach and are interested in learning why and how users interact, understand, and use/misuse security and privacy tools.
