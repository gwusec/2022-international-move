International immigration means that people leave their “known” environment and have to interact with new entities, often sharing a lot of sensitive information in a short amount of time. Usually, “adulting" and community help understand how to navigate these. However, when changing the environment radically, these “usual” pathways to navigate data sharing and whether or not something is a “scam” may be a lot harder.

We are interested in understanding the challenges that international immigrants to the US face and what measures they undertake to protect their security and privacy. We are also interested to learn about advice they receive and what security implications they entail.

Data Handling #

Your data will be de-identified. The focus group sessions and interviews will be audio-recorded and transcripted. This allows us to capture all details and analyze the data better. If you don’t feel comfortable being recorded, please let us know….

About Us #

We are a group of researchers from the Usable Security and Privacy Lab at The George Washington University (GWUSEC). We work on problems related to computer security and privacy from a human centered approach.
